Document 16 · Research · The Founder's Verdict

If I were starting a Ghana real estate business today — here's the one I'd build.

Not a menu of options — a decision. A panel of local and international experts each names the area they'd bet on, backed by the data. Then it's synthesised into one business, ranked focus areas, and the first 90 days.

◆ The verdict, up front
Build an asset-light, trust-led build-and-stay services company — one that manages, supplies, procures and finances other people's property, and owns almost none of it. Own the flow, not the buildings. The developer route and the listings war are both traps.

The Panel · Tap Each Expert

Eight experts. Each names one area to bet on.

A working advisory board — half on the ground in Ghana, half from mature markets. Each gives a single verdict and the data behind it. Local (gold) and international (blue).

The Synthesis · The Business We'd Build

One company, four connected lines, almost no owned property

The panel converges. The business isn't a developer and isn't a portal — it's the trusted operating layer that sits between Ghanaians (home and abroad) and the fragmented, low-trust building and property market. It earns fees on activity, not risk on inventory.

Why asset-light, decisively

Owning and developing property is a capital trap for a new entrant — slow, cash-hungry, and exposed to cedi and construction risk. The data shows the return in the best segment (short-let, 15–25% gross) is captured by operating skill, not the deed — the top 10% of listings out-earn the median 3–4×. So we sell the skill, the trust and the access, and let clients (and later, our own reinvested cash) own the bricks.

The Focus Areas · Ranked

Where attention and money go — in order

1

Short-let management

The flagship · highest yield

Run diaspora- and middle-class-owned units as professional short-lets for a 15–25% fee. Zero property capital; recurring cash; solves the client's empty-house problem.

Data: short-let 15–25% gross (top category) · Accra 2,200+ active listings · fees 15–25% of gross
2

Materials commerce + diaspora procurement

The cash & trust engine

Group-buy materials (below-retail via pooled volume) and fee-based remote build management for the diaspora. The high-trust base that feeds everything.

Data: ~90% self-build · mortgage 0.8% · the "completeness gap" stalls builds · diaspora remittances $6.65B
3

Bank finance partnerships

Margin on activity

Refer pre-qualified, lower-risk borrowers to banks for construction and home-improvement loans; earn origination commission. Pure margin, no capital.

Data: Reference Rate 15.68% (best in 4+ yrs) · high sector NPLs = banks pay for quality leads
4

Selective land banking

The wealth store · later

Deploy accumulated profits into peri-urban land in confirmed infrastructure corridors. Appreciation, no operations — the store of value, not the day-one business.

Data: peri-urban land 50–200% over 5–10 yrs · verify title via Lands Commission (fraud risk)
The connective tissue

All four sit on one trust/media brand — the content engine that makes Ghanaians choose Wima before anyone else. The brand is the customer-acquisition moat; the four lines are how that trust is monetised.

The Discipline

What we'd focus on — and what we'd refuse to do

✓ Focus
  • Operating skill & trust — the scarce, defensible assets
  • Recurring, fee-based income — management, procurement, commission
  • The audience as supply — clients' empty houses become managed inventory
  • USD-earning lines (diaspora, foreign short-let guests) as a cedi hedge
  • Greater Accra + Kumasi first — 65% of transactions, the demand core
✕ Refuse (at least at first)
  • Becoming a developer — capital trap, slow, cedi & build risk
  • The listings war — meQasa & Ownkey own it; don't fund a losing fight
  • Buying property before operating it — earn the knowledge first
  • Arbitrage at scale — fixed rent, no equity, no cushion (see Sonder)
  • Spreading thin — one wedge, won completely, before expanding

Honest framing

This is a data-informed strategic view, not a guarantee or financial advice. Returns depend on execution, market conditions, currency, and disciplined operation in the top quartile — not the median. Validate every figure against your own results and a qualified adviser before committing capital.

The one line to hold

A new entrant can't out-capital the developers or out-list the portals. But nobody has out-trusted the market on how to build and stay. Build the trusted operating layer first — own the relationship, not the real estate — and the assets become something you buy later, cheaply, on your own terms.
