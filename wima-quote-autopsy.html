The verdict

Same room. The vague quote is GHS 350 higher — and that's before the day-of surprises the "extras" line exists to allow. But the money isn't even the main point. With the itemised BOQ you know the grade of tile, the quantity, and the labour cost — so you can compare it against any other quote and check it against reality. With the vague one, you're trusting a number with nothing behind it. An honest quote has nothing to hide, so it hides nothing.

What the autopsy reveals

  • No area, no product grade. "Ceiling for your room" could be premium acoustic panels or the cheapest tile on the market — at the same price. The grade is the cost. Substitution risk
  • Materials as one lump. A single "materials: GHS 2,400" can't be checked against the market. Itemised, you'd see each price. Padding hides here
  • Labour "included." Merged with materials, you can't tell what either really costs — or whether labour is fair. Un-checkable
  • "Extras" and transport undefined. A vague buffer line is where the day-of surprises live. Open-ended
  • No wastage stated. The honest BOQ shows the 8% offcut allowance openly instead of burying it in an inflated quantity. Opacity
  • No written timeline. "Small small" is not a start and end date — and it's how a job stretches for weeks. No accountability
