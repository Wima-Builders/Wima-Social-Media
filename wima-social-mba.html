The Dean's Note · How This Program Works

Four principles borrowed from the best business schools

1 · The case method

Harvard teaches through cases, not lectures. Here, Wima is the living case. Every concept is applied to a real decision — the Dry Wall launch, the diaspora funnel, the bank pitch — so theory never floats free of practice.

2 · Learn by shipping

You don't pass a course by reading it. You pass by producing the asset — a content calendar, a live campaign, a LinkedIn thought-leadership series. The deliverable is the exam.

3 · AI-native, human-led

AI does 70–80% of the production; your judgment does the 20–30% that matters — strategy, taste, ethics, and the relationship. You'll master the tools without outsourcing the thinking.

4 · Ethics as foundation

Wima's entire moat is trust. Every growth tactic in this program is pressure-tested against one question: does it build trust or spend it? Nothing that burns the brand is worth the reach.

The Wima starter stack (lean & cheap)

You don't need the enterprise tools. Start with: ChatGPT or Claude (strategy + scripts) → Canva (visuals) → CapCut (video editing, free) → OpusClip or Vizard (turn one long video into a week of clips) → Submagic (captions that hold attention) → Buffer or Metricool (schedule + analytics) → Supergrow (LinkedIn). That whole stack runs cheap and covers 95% of what Wima needs.

Special Focus · LinkedIn & B2B Growth

The rail that reaches the people who write cheques

TikTok and WhatsApp reach the builder. LinkedIn reaches everyone who makes Wima's ecosystem work — and almost no Ghanaian building brand is using it seriously. That's the opening.

Who's actually on LinkedIn for Wima

  • Bank decision-makers — the partnerships and mortgage leads for your finance rail live here. Warm them before you pitch.
  • Professionals — architects, QSs, engineers, contractors for the vetted-execution network and tendering supply side.
  • Suppliers & manufacturers — Deal Hub and tender partners.
  • Diaspora professionals — high-income Ghanaians abroad who build — the premium client, reachable by their job, not just their nationality.

What to do there

  • Found-led thought leadership. Gershon posts the building-industry insights from these very documents. The founder's voice builds B2B trust faster than a brand page.
  • Document the journey. Ecosystem thinking, market data, launch lessons — the strategy work becomes content.
  • Warm outreach, not cold. Comment on and engage bank/industry leaders for weeks before any ask. Relationship first.
  • Carousels & data posts. LinkedIn rewards useful, native carousels — perfect for your price data and market insights.
The B2B rhythm

LinkedIn is a slow, compounding trust channel — post 2–3× a week, engage daily, and think in months not days. Its payoff isn't viral reach; it's that when you finally message a bank's partnership lead, they already know your name and respect your thinking. That's worth more than a thousand cold impressions.
