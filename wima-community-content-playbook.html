Document 19 · The Consolidated Playbook · Community · Content · Network

Build the community first. Everything else is switched on later.

One playbook for the whole scheme: why community — not deals — is the asset, how content builds it, how we quietly recruit the professionals and master craftsmen who power it, and how to use other creators' content without legal trouble. The rails come after the crowd.

The core principle: a feed of discounts attracts bargain-hunters who leave when the deal ends. A feed of genuinely useful, beautiful content builds a community that stays — and then buys. We earn the crowd with content, and monetise it later through tendering, Deal Hub, flash sales, and big-ticket procurement.

Part 1 · Community First

The community is the asset. The rails are the return.

This is the Bezos / Jack Ma move: own the audience and the trust, and the revenue lines become almost inevitable afterwards. We are not launching a marketplace and hoping people come — we are building a community, then opening the shop inside it.

Now · Build
The community & the trust. Content, the vetted network, the WhatsApp groups, the diaspora relationships. This is the whole job right now.
Later · Monetise
Tendering. A live community of builders & pros makes the toll-road liquid from day one.
Later · Monetise
Deal Hub & flash sales. Group-buys and time-boxed drops land hard on an audience that already trusts you.
Later · Monetise
Big-ticket procurement. Local and international suppliers — the community's aggregated demand is the negotiating power.
Why sequence it this way

Every monetisation rail needs liquidity — enough people on both sides to work. A community built on trust supplies that liquidity instantly when you switch a rail on. Try to monetise before the community exists and every rail launches into an empty room. Crowd first; commerce second.

Part 2 · Content, Not Just Deals

Deals are what you do to a community — content is what builds one

If the feed is all promotions, you train people to only show up for discounts. If the feed is genuinely useful and beautiful, you train them to belong. Here are the content pillars that build belonging.

Pillar 01 · Educate

Teach them to build well

Cost breakdowns, the build order, land verification, "mistakes that cost you twice." The trusted guide the market never had.

Pillar 02 · Inspire

Show the dream, real

Before/after, finished homes, site reveals, beautiful projects. The transformation content the whole industry runs on.

Pillar 03 · People

Honour the makers

The consultants' projects and the master craftsmen's skill. Real experts, real hands — the human story behind the build.

Pillar 04 · Deals

Then, the offer

Group-buys, flash sales, launches — used sparingly, landing on an audience that already values you. The smallest pillar, not the biggest.

The ratio to hold

Roughly three parts value (educate, inspire, people) to one part offer. Earn attention far more than you spend it. The community you build with the first three pillars is what makes the fourth pillar convert at all.

Part 3 · The Professionals & Master Craftsmen Network

Recruited quietly, by recommendation — never by advert

Ghana's built-environment professionals can't advertise themselves under their conduct codes — but they can be engaged and featured. We turn that constraint into a talent moat, and we do it discreetly: trusted referrals, not a loud campaign.

Tier 1 · Professionals

Featured as Consultants

Architects, engineers, quantity surveyors — engaged as Wima consultants, their projects featured as "projects by our vetted professionals." Compliant visibility they cannot get any other way, in exchange for real project flow. We recruit them one warm introduction at a time.

Tier 2 · Master craftsmen

Marketed directly

Master masons, tilers, carpenters, steel-benders — not bound by the same restrictions, and the subject of a national push to certify them. We showcase their craft, build their reputations, and route work to them. The human, authentic heart of the content.

Why quiet recruitment is the right move

Discretion is what keeps it dignified for the professional and clean on compliance — they are being honoured and invited, never seen responding to an advert. A recommendation from a peer they respect converts far better than any pitch, and it self-selects for quality. The network grows by trust, the same currency the whole business runs on.

How the network powers everything

The consultants and craftsmen are simultaneously the supply behind vetted execution and tendering, the credibility that makes Wima's marketing believable, and the source of the project content that fills the feed. One relationship, three returns. Monetised later through project fees, materials pull-through, and the tendering take-rate — never by charging them to be featured.

The compliance line to hold

Feature work as editorial case studies and genuine consultant engagements — never "pay us to promote you," which would edge into paid self-advertising. Confirm the exact wording with GIA / GhIE / GhIS. This is strategy, not legal advice — have a Ghanaian professional-practice lawyer bless the framing before scaling.

Part 4 · Using Other Creators' Content — Legally

The rights-clearance ladder

Great curation multiplies your content without a studio — but only if it's done cleanly. Tap each rung, safest at the top. Default to the top of the ladder; the lower you go, the more care you need.

Referencing professionally — every time

✓Credit fully — name, handle, and a link back to the original.
✓Label it honestly — "Project by [architect]," "Shared with permission," "Featured consultant."
✓Keep the receipt — save the written "yes" (a DM reply counts) for anything you re-post.
✕Never imply a partnership or endorsement that doesn't exist.
✕Never edit someone's work in a way that changes its meaning.
Three traps to internalise

1 · Assume everything online is copyrighted by its creator — the safe default is permission or native resharing, never quiet re-uploading. 2 · Don't rely on "fair use" / fair dealing as a strategy — Ghana's Copyright Act 2005 (Act 690) exceptions are narrow, fact-specific, and get risky the moment money is involved. 3 · Music is the most common trap — only ever use audio from the platform's own licensed library; copyrighted songs on a business account get videos muted, pulled, or claimed.

Make it repeatable

Have a Ghanaian IP lawyer bless one standard content-usage clause (for consultant & craftsmen agreements) and one short clearance checklist for the social team. Do it once, and the whole community-content engine runs safely on repeat.

Part 5 · What The Community Unlocks

The scale-ups the crowd makes possible

Everything above is in service of this: a community large and trusting enough that these rails launch into a full room, not an empty one.

Rail · liquid on day one

Tendering

A standing community of builders and vetted pros means bids appear the moment a project is posted. The toll-road works because the traffic already exists.

Rail · demand on tap

Deal Hub & flash sales

Group-buys and time-boxed drops convert hard on an audience that trusts you — urgency lands because the relationship is already there.

Rail · the big prize

Big-ticket procurement

Aggregated community demand is the negotiating power to procure at scale — from local and international suppliers, at prices no individual could reach.

The through-line

We are not building a shop and hoping for a crowd. We are building the crowd — with content, trust, and the people who make things — and the shop opens inside it. Community first; the rails are simply what the community makes possible.
