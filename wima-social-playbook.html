Two audiences, one trust brand: the local builder and the one abroad.

The research is clear on what works on social in Ghana — and just as clear that Wima's two core audiences fear different things. The middle-class self-builder fears running out of money and being cheated on price. The diaspora fears being betrayed from a distance. Serve both, on the same trust-first channels, with different messages.

Part 2 · Two Audiences, Two Fears

Same channels. Different pain. Different message.

Both live on WhatsApp, both buy on trust, both respond to video. But what keeps each awake at night is completely different — so the content and the offer must be too.

◆ Audience 1 · The local middle class

The self-builder on the ground

The ~90% who build incrementally with their own cash. They can visit the site — so distance isn't the issue. Money and knowledge are.

Core fear: "I'll run out of money half-built, and I'm being overcharged because I don't know what things should cost."

◆ Audience 2 · The diaspora

The builder abroad

Higher-value, USD-earning, building remotely. They have the money — what they lack is visibility and trustworthy hands on the ground.

Core fear: "The people I trusted with my money — even family — will betray me, and I won't see it happen."

Why serve both — and how they overlap

The middle class is the volume; the diaspora is the value. And they frequently connect: a diaspora relative often co-funds a local family member's build. Win the local builder and you're often already in the diaspora's WhatsApp. One brand, one trust engine, two tuned messages.

Part 4 · How To Reach Each Audience

Different people, in different places

◆ Reaching the local middle class

TikTok & Facebook — local reach
Geo: Accra · Tema · Kumasi

Build-education video is the mass discovery engine. Cost breakdowns, mistakes, "how much does X cost." Target by local geography + build interest.

Deal Hub deals as the hook
The acquisition magnet

The group-buy price offer is what pulls the price-sensitive builder in. Lead ads and deal drops to local audiences.

WhatsApp builders' group
The community & close

Regional builder groups, deal-drop broadcasts, Q&A. Where the local builder asks questions and buys.

Local relevance & artisans
Neighbourhood + trust

Neighbourhood cost content, cedi/material-price news, and local masons/contractors as trusted voices.

◆ Reaching the diaspora

Meta geo-targeted ads
Geo: UK · US · Canada

Target Ghanaians abroad by location + interest. Click-to-WhatsApp. Location-based ads reach overseas builders without leaving Accra.

LinkedIn — high-income diaspora
Reach them by their job

Diaspora professionals (doctors, engineers, finance). Founder-led thought leadership builds the authority that converts them.

Diaspora groups & associations
Community reach

Hometown, church & diaspora Facebook groups. Show up with value (ethical ambush), not spam.

Founder as the face
Trust is personal

The diaspora buys from a person. Gershon fronting the education outperforms a faceless brand.

The content behaviour that wins both

Show, don't tell — on video. For the local builder, a transparent live cost breakdown kills the "am I being overcharged?" fear. For the diaspora, scheduled site & drone updates kill the "am I being cheated from afar?" fear. Same behaviour — visible proof — answering two different fears.

Part 5 · The Two Funnels

Each audience travels a different path to the same trust

◆ Middle-class funnel · price & certainty

1 · Discover
Cost & mistakes video. "The quote that left out 30% of your budget" stops the scroll.
2 · Educate
Close the completeness gap. True costs, the build order, what to buy when.
3 · Deal
Deal Hub / tender. Group-buy price leverage — the "no longer overcharged" moment.
4 · Execute
Vetted pros. Trusted execution + a discount when they build with Wima.
5 · Finance
Bank facility. The way to finish in 18 months instead of stalling for a decade.

◆ Diaspora funnel · fear to trust

1 · Reach
Geo video + LinkedIn. A "biggest diaspora mistake" story. They feel seen.
2 · Educate
Answer the fear. Verification, milestone payments, "don't rely on family alone."
3 · Prove
Show, don't tell. Real project video updates, drone footage, a client's story.
4 · Relationship
Into WhatsApp. The diaspora support group, time-zone-aware, a real person.
5 · Serve
Procurement Desk delivers. Milestone payments, scheduled updates, vetted execution.
The one line to hold

To the local builder, Wima says: "You'll know exactly what it costs, and you won't be overcharged." To the diaspora, Wima says: "You'll see every step, and you won't be betrayed." Both are promises of the same thing — the certainty this market has never had.
