Signature Content Franchises · Two to run now · Built on what competitors can't copy

Two franchises. Not six.

At 2–3 clips a week, six recurring series means each appears twice a month — that's a rumour, not a franchise. So we focus on two that don't compete for the same resource and feed two different products: one spends Jamal's site time and sells drywall now; one spends your desk time and builds the Deal Hub audience for next. Everything else is queued behind an asset you don't have yet.

The Rule Why These Two

Stand out only on what they can't follow you into

Every contractor in Accra claims quality and trust. The reason radical transparency works for Wima isn't that it's nice — it's that competitors' margin depends on opacity. They structurally cannot copy it. Both franchises below are built on that principle, and each one attaches to a different product and a different budget.

Franchise A — spends Jamal's site time

Cool Before AC

Film you're already shooting. Reaches people at the finishing stage — exactly where Dry Wall with Jamal sells. This is your Month 1 engine.

Franchise B — spends your desk time

The Cost Trail

Written, no camera. Reaches people at the land & permit stage — who are ~6 weeks from buying cement. This is the Deal Hub audience, built in advance. Your Month 2 engine.

The disciplineOne franchise for the product you're launching, one for the product you're launching next. Two different budgets (Jamal's camera vs your keyboard) so they never fight for the same hour. If you could only run one: Cool Before AC — revenue now beats authority later when you're launching.

Franchise B · Month 2 · Deal Hub audience

The Cost Trail

One government land/building institution decoded per week — but every post ends on the BOQ line it affects. Everyone else explains the process; only Wima prices it, because only Wima publishes itemised BOQs.
Format
Carousel / written (WhatsApp + Facebook + LinkedIn). No camera. Your desk time.
Areas
Lands Commission (the search) · LUSPA (planning) · District Assembly (permit) · TDC · Ghana Standards Authority (fake-cement) · EPA · Rent Control · GRA (stamp duty) · customary land · Land Act 2020.

The Wima twist: it's not a Paper Trail, it's a Cost Trail — paperwork priced as build cost. Uncopyable in practice: tedious, unglamorous, no sale at the end, so no competitor chasing a quote will bother. And it hooks the diaspora on their single biggest fear.

Before You Run Them Non-Negotiable

Two cautions that protect the moat

The Cost Trail can hurt someonePermit and land processes change, and wrong information costs a real person real money. Verify against each institution's own published source, cite it, date the post, and state plainly it isn't legal advice. If you can't sustain that verification, don't run this franchise — a silent series is survivable; a wrong one that misleads a buyer is not. And never republish a scare stat from a firm selling the fix — the inflated land-fraud percentages are exactly that. Primary source or cut the number.
Cool Before AC — get the facts and photos rightBuilding dates, neighbourhood histories and cooling claims get mangled in retelling. Verify before you publish. And don't scrape photos — a Wima-shot image of a Cantonments street is worth more than a borrowed one, and it's yours. Your whole moat is being the one who doesn't exaggerate; one wrong post costs more than fifty missed ones.
