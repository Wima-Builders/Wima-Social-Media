01 The Standards

Nine rules we don't break.

The difference between a company that posts and a media team that wins isn't budget or talent — it's a small set of non-negotiables held under pressure. These are Wima's.

Rule 01

Hook or die

You have three seconds. 71% of viewers decide almost immediately whether to keep watching. No logo intros, no "hi guys, welcome back." The first frame is the whole job.

Rule 02

Volume beats polish

A smartphone clip with a sharp hook outperforms a professionally produced film with a weak one. Never let production quality become the excuse for not shipping.

Rule 03

One idea per asset

One post, one point, one CTA. If it needs two ideas, it's two posts. Confusion is the enemy of conversion.

Rule 04

Native, never cross-posted

The same message, re-written for each platform's job. A TikTok pasted to LinkedIn insults both audiences. The 60-Day Engine exists precisely for this.

Rule 05

Systems over inspiration

Nobody waits to feel creative. One shoot a week on a live job, 2–3 clips out, a week ahead. Consistency beats volume — three posts every week for a year beats ten posts one week and silence the next.

Rule 06

Test hooks like headlines

Same video, different opening. Post both, compare 48-hour retention. Winners become templates; the hook library grows. Guessing is banned.

Rule 07

Never pay to push a flop

Paid doesn't fix weak creative — it buys reach for it. Only content that already earned organic traction gets budget. Organic is the lab; paid is the amplifier.

Rule 08

Measure the business, not the vanity

Views are not the goal. Shares and WhatsApp messages started are. A 200-view post that starts 6 conversations beats a 20,000-view post that starts none.

Rule 09

Real, or don't post it

Audiences detect templated, generic, AI-flavoured content instantly — and in a trust market that's fatal. Real sites, real Jamal, real clients, real numbers. Authenticity is the strategy.

"If it comes for price, it leaves for price. If it comes for trust, it stays." — the line under every decision here.

03 The Short-Video System

Short video is the whole game now.

85% of marketers name short-form the most effective format of 2026, and it carries the highest ROI of any social content. For Wima it's close to unfair: every job site is a film set, every install is a transformation, and Jamal is a character people will follow. Here's the system that turns that into reach.

The hook library — tap a type

Nine hook patterns that work for building content, each with Wima-ready lines. Steal these verbatim; the first three seconds decide everything.

The production pipeline — one shoot, ten assets

The single highest-leverage habit in modern content. Don't produce videos one at a time — film once on a live Jamal job and harvest the week from it. One shoot a week, 2–3 clips published. Run it 8–12 weeks and you stop guessing.

Step 01

Record raw

15–30 minutes on a real site: the shell, the frame, the boards, the joint, the reveal, Jamal talking. Phone only. No script.

Step 02

Cut 5–10 clips

Each 15–30 seconds, one idea, vertical, captions burned in (most watch sound-off). Different hook on each.

Step 03

Publish 2–3

Post organically across TikTok / Reels / Shorts / Status. Different days, different hooks. Let the audience vote. 2–3 a week is the real target — sustainable beats heroic.

Step 04

Promote 1–2

Take whichever earned real organic traction and put budget behind it as a click-to-WhatsApp ad. Never the flops.

Step 05

Bank the winners

Save every hook that worked into the library and re-run it next week on new footage. The system compounds.

The craft rules that decide distribution15–30 seconds is the sweet spot — TikTok rewards completion rate above all, so shorter usually wins. Vertical, always. Captions burned in — assume sound is off. Loop the ending into the beginning where you can; re-watches lift retention. Hook → value → one CTA, nothing else. And every single one ends on WhatsApp.
04 Organic × Paid

Organic is the lab. Paid is the amplifier.

Most companies get this backwards — they invent an ad, then hope. World-class teams let the audience pick the winners for free, then pay to scale only what already proved itself. That discipline is worth more than a bigger budget.

Non-paid · the lab

What organic is for

  • Discovery & testing. Publish 3 clips a week with different hooks and let real people vote before a pesewa is spent.
  • Community & trust. Facebook groups, WhatsApp Status, comment replies — Gershon present, as a person, not a page.
  • Proof accumulation. Every finished job becomes a before/after, a testimonial, a case study you own forever.
  • Free reach on relationships. Platforms now favour the interest graph over the follower graph — engagement, not follower count, decides distribution.
  • The rule: a post that earns shares and messages organically is a proven ad. One that doesn't, never will be.
Paid · the amplifier

What paid is for

  • Click-to-WhatsApp is the money format. Not traffic, not likes — the ad opens a conversation on the channel where Wima closes.
  • Two ad sets, always. Local (Accra / Tema / Kumasi) and diaspora (UK / US / Canada / Germany) — different fears, different creative, never merged.
  • Beat ad fatigue with a 3×2×2 matrix: 3 hooks × 2 edits × 2 CTAs = 12 variants from one shoot. Refresh before performance decays.
  • Retarget the warm. People who watched 50%+ of a video or messaged and went quiet get the free-BOQ offer. Cheapest conversions you'll ever buy.
  • The metric that matters: cost per WhatsApp message started — not CPM, not CTR. Kill fast, scale the winner.
The amplification ladder — how budget actually gets spent1 · Warm first. Run organic for ~a week before any spend — never advertise into a cold, empty page. 2 · Small tests. Put a small daily budget behind 2–3 proven organic clips; run 3–5 days. 3 · Kill fast. Anything with a poor cost-per-message-started dies without sentiment. 4 · Scale the winner gradually, and refresh creative before fatigue sets in. 5 · Retarget the warm audience with the free BOQ. Repeat every cycle.
05 Campaign · Deal Hub Launch

Launching Deal Hub without becoming Groupon.

The trap is real: a discount platform trains price-hunters who leave for the next discount. So Deal Hub doesn't launch as a discount — it launches as a collective. The story isn't "cheap cement," it's "builders who buy together stop being at the mercy of the market." Four phases; tap each.

The one creative decision that protects the whole thingNever lead with the discount. Lead with the mechanic — "alone you pay retail; together we buy at volume" — and with the pool count rising. The saving is the result of belonging, not a coupon you hand a stranger. That single framing is the difference between Infra.Market (~$2B on aggregation) and Groupon (−$1.14B on discounts).
06 The Online Community

The community is the asset.

Everything else Wima builds sits on this. 76% of consumers are more likely to buy from brands with a strong community, active communities see 53% higher retention, and community-driven brands get roughly 5× the engagement through members' own content. For Deal Hub it's not a nice-to-have — a group-buy without a group is just a price list.

The architecture

Built for your actual capacity — 2–3 clips a week, best-effort repliesThis blueprint is deliberately phased, not full-fat. Open two rooms and two rituals now; add the rest when Deal Hub launches and when demand genuinely shows. A silent, unanswered group does more damage than no group at all — it publicly proves the brand doesn't follow through, which is the exact opposite of what Wima is selling. Two rooms you run well beat four you run badly. Grow the architecture behind the demand, never ahead of it.

WhatsApp Communities let you hold several focused groups under one umbrella with an announcement channel on top — order instead of chaos, which is exactly what a builders' community needs. Start with the two marked Start now.

The growth loop — why this compounds

Loop 01

Value pulls people in

Free BOQ, price updates, clinic answers. No purchase required, ever. Content is the front door.

Loop 02

Belonging keeps them

Recognition by name, their build featured, their question answered. Members become regulars, regulars become advocates.

Loop 03

Scale lowers the price

A bigger pool = a better Deal Hub price for everyone. Members are rewarded for recruiting — the incentive is structural, not begged for.

Loop 04

Savings prove the value

Real cedis saved → testimonials → more members → an even bigger pool. The loop tightens with every cycle.

The rules that keep it aliveConsent only — nobody is added without asking; opt-out is one message and honoured instantly. Gershon is present — a founder in the room beats any community manager; people detect an absent brand. Publish your real response window and beat it — "we reply within a few hours, Mon–Sat" kept is worth ten times "instant" broken. Never promise a speed you can't hold on a busy site day. Recognise the regulars by name — recognition builds loyalty, loyalty builds momentum, and AkomaPoints will later make it formal. No spam, no poaching, no fake materials — one bad batch or one hustler destroys the moat you spent months building. Never blast the announcement rail for anything that isn't genuinely useful.
07 The Scoreboard

What we count — and what we ignore.

Most teams drown in dashboards that flatter them. Wima tracks a short list that maps to cedis and trust, reviewed every Sunday. Winners shape the next week; everything else is noise.

◆ Count these
  • WhatsApp messages started — the only true top-of-funnel conversion. Everything routes here.
  • Shares & saves — the honest signal of value. A share is someone staking their reputation on you.
  • 3-second retention & completion rate — did the hook hold? This is the creative scoreboard.
  • Cost per message started — the only paid metric that matters. Kill anything expensive, fast.
  • Community: active contributors (not member count) and pool participation rate.
  • Free BOQs requested → quoted → won — the funnel, end to end.
  • Repeat & referral rate — the truest measure of whether trust is real.
✕ Ignore these
  • Views & impressions — reach without a conversation is a rounding error.
  • Follower count — platforms now distribute on interest and engagement, not on your follower number.
  • Likes — the cheapest, least predictive signal there is.
  • Member count — a silent group of 2,000 is worth less than 200 who talk and buy together.
  • CPM / CTR in isolation — cheap clicks that never message are just cheap.
  • Posting streaks — volume without learning is just noise with a calendar.
The Sunday review — 20 minutes, every weekRank the week's posts by shares and messages started. Ask three questions: which hook won and why; which two clips deserve budget this week; and what did the community actually ask for? Then build next week around the answers. Eight to twelve weeks of this loop and you'll stop guessing — you'll know.
08 The Backbone

Six pillars. Every post has a job.

Whatever you post, from whichever asset, it belongs to one of these — and each answers a documented client fear. Roughly three parts value to one part offer, always.

Educate

Costs, build order, reading a BOQ. → Hidden costs & bad data.

Inspire

Before/after, reveals. → "Will it be worth it?"

People

Jamal, the craftsmen. → "Who's on my site?"

Entertain

Dream builds, wild reveals. → Reach offers can't buy.

Proof

Real clients, voice notes. → The fear of betrayal.

Offer

Free BOQ, Deal Hub drops. → The smallest pillar.

Before you post — the two habits that protect everythingVerify every number. Prices, timelines, temperature drops and [X] placeholders across all assets are illustrative — replace them with your real, current figures. A wrong price does more damage to Wima than a missed post. Always get consent. Never use a client's photo, name, area or voice note without an explicit yes, and never publish a savings or outcome claim you can't evidence from a real install. Wima's entire moat is being the one who doesn't exaggerate.
