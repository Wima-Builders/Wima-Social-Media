Document 14 · Strategy · The Supply Side of Deal Hub

We don't ask suppliers for a favour — we bring them demand.

Deal Hub only works if suppliers, distributors and manufacturers want in. The way to win them is simple: arrive with aggregated demand and take the cost and hassle of selling off their plate. Here's how we introduce ourselves, and how we onboard them with almost no friction.

The whole idea in one line: to a supplier, Wima is not another buyer haggling on price — Wima is a demand channel that delivers pooled orders, new customers, and reliable settlement, at no marketing cost to them. That's an easy yes.

        Part 2 · How We Introduce Ourselves

        Five principles for the first conversation

        01 · Frame

        Channel, not customer

        Open as a demand partner, not a buyer negotiating price. "We bring you orders" beats "give us a discount."

        02 · Proof

        Lead with the demand

        Show the community and pipeline — the builders, the diaspora, the pending group-buys. Numbers, not promises.

        03 · Warm

        Referred, not cold

        An introduction from a known distributor, trade contact or industry name lands far better than a cold call.

        04 · Pilot

        Start with one deal

        Propose a single group-buy to prove the volume is real. A completed first order does the convincing.

        05 · Respect

        Protect their margins

        We win on volume, not by squeezing them thin. A supplier who profits with us stays and refers others.

        06 · Easy

        No tech barrier to start

        First deal runs on WhatsApp and a simple price list — not a portal they must learn. Ease is the offer.

        Sample first-contact message (adapt per supplier)

        Hello [Name], I'm reaching out from Wima Builders. We run a fast-growing community of Ghanaian builders and diaspora clients who buy building materials together, in volume, through group deals.

        Rather than ask you for a discount, we'd like to bring you pooled orders — aggregated demand from many buyers as a single, reliable purchase, with no marketing cost on your side.

        Could we start with one pilot deal on [product]? If you can share your best volume price for [quantity], we'll take it to our community and place the combined order. If it works for both sides, we grow from there.

        Happy to send a one-page overview or meet whenever suits you. — [Your name], Wima Builders

        Part 3 · Onboarding With Ease

        Six low-friction steps from "hello" to first order

        The onboarding is deliberately light. Every step removes friction, because a supplier who onboards in a day is a supplier who stays.

        1

        Introduce & qualify

        Warm intro or targeted outreach; confirm they carry the products our community wants and can meet volume.

        Channel: WhatsApp / call · same day
        2

        Simple profile

        Capture the essentials only: company, products, volume pricing, delivery areas, lead times, payment terms. A short form — not paperwork.

        A one-page form, not a portal
        3

        Light vetting

        Verify they're registered and their materials are genuine — the quality check that protects the community's trust.

        Protects the brand promise
        4

        Agree simple terms

        A short supplier agreement: volume pricing tiers, settlement terms, quality standard, delivery responsibility. Clear and fair.

        One-page agreement template
        5

        Run the pilot deal

        List one product in a group-buy, aggregate the orders, place the combined order, deliver. Proof, not promises.

        The trust-builder
        6

        Scale & integrate

        After a successful pilot, add more products, more frequent deals, and — later — deeper catalog integration and priority status.

        Grows into a real partnership
        The onboarding principle

        Start tech-light and prove value before asking for effort. The supplier's first experience should be a completed, profitable order, not a form marathon. Systems and integration come after trust, never before it.

        Part 4 · Two Tracks, Sequenced

        Local first, international next

        ◆ Track 1 · Now — for the Deal Hub launch

        Local distributors & manufacturers

        Faster logistics, simpler terms, easier trust-building. These suppliers power the first Deal Hub deals (starting with the Dry Wall launch).

        Approach: direct relationships, WhatsApp, warm intros, small pilots. Cedi pricing, local delivery. Win a handful of reliable ones and prove the model.

        ◆ Track 2 · Later — the big-ticket scale-up

        International suppliers & manufacturers

        For big-ticket, differentiated procurement once the community's aggregated demand is large enough to justify container-scale orders.

        Approach: Wima as their trusted Ghana channel — one partner handling demand, import, forex and last-mile, so they access the market without setting up locally. Consolidated demand is the hook.

        Why this order

        International suppliers ship for scale you don't yet have. Win local suppliers first, build the community and the order volume, and the aggregated demand becomes the very thing that makes international manufacturers take your call. The community earns the international deals.

        Part 5 · The Guardrail

        Genuine materials, or nothing

        Non-negotiable

        Fake cement, weak rods and substandard blocks are one of the market's deepest fears — and one wave of complaints would undo the trust Wima is built on. Every supplier is vetted for genuine, quality materials before a single deal. The volume we offer is the leverage to demand authenticity. Trust is the product; suppliers who can't protect it don't get the demand.

        The one line to hold

        Suppliers don't want another buyer haggling them down — they want reliable demand without the cost of chasing it. Bring them the community's pooled orders, onboard them in a day, protect their margin and our quality, and the supply side builds itself.
