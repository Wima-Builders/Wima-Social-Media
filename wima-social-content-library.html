Section 01 The Strategy

Six Content Pillars —
and why they beat random posting.

Random posts build nothing. A pillar system builds a community that recognises Wima, trusts it, and buys when it's ready. The rule underneath all six: roughly three parts value (educate, inspire, people, entertain, proof) to one part offer. Deals land only on a community you've already earned.

Pillar 01 · Value
Educate
Teach people to build well and not get cheated — cost breakdowns, the build order, "mistakes that cost you twice," how to read a BOQ.
e.g. "5 checks before you pour your foundation"
Pillar 02 · Value
Inspire
The dream, made real. Before/after transformations, finished reveals, site walk-throughs. The single most-shared content the home industry runs on.
e.g. bare concrete → acoustic ceiling reveal
Pillar 03 · Value
People
Honour the makers. Jamal the drywall craftsman, master masons and tilers, featured consultants. People follow people — put a human face on Wima.
e.g. "Meet Jamal — 12 years, every board perfect"
Pillar 04 · Value
Entertain
Make browsing fun (the Zillow lesson). Dream builds, jaw-dropping Ghanaian homes, "you won't believe this" — content window-shoppers share, not just buyers.
e.g. "Ghana builds gone wild" reveals
Pillar 05 · Value
Proof
Real clients, real numbers, real quotes. Case studies, testimonials, voice notes. In Ghana, social proof and word-of-mouth beat any advert.
e.g. "No wahala with these guys" — real client
Pillar 06 · Offer
Offer
The smallest pillar. Deal Hub drops, free-BOQ CTAs, WhatsApp-first launches — used sparingly, landing on an audience that already values Wima.
e.g. "Community gets this cement deal first"
The one rule that ties them togetherFor every offer post, publish three that give pure value. A feed of discounts trains bargain-hunters who vanish when the deal ends; a feed of value trains a community that stays — and then buys. Every post, whatever the pillar, ends with a single WhatsApp call-to-action: interest starts on social, deals close on WhatsApp.
Section 02 Learn From The World's Best

What the global leaders do —
and Wima's Ghana version.

The home-and-property giants already solved social. We don't copy their market — we copy their mechanics. Each lesson below is proven at scale and adapted to Accra, Kumasi, and the diaspora. This section doubles as a case-method module in the Social Media MBA.

HouzzUSA · 40M+ users
What they did
Turned keyword-tagged project photos into a searchable library that ranks on Google for years, and made answering questions its own content — pros who help win reach and authority.
Wima's Ghana version
Tag every before/after and reveal with build-intent keywords so the feed becomes evergreen, searchable proof. Be the most useful voice in Accra Facebook groups and WhatsApp Q&A — every answer is a trust deposit and free reach.
LivspaceIndia · Ghana-like market
What they did
Dramatised the pain ("Don't Try This At Home" — disappearing contractors, creeping costs) for ~40% more leads, then sold WhatsApp-first with catalogs and ran seasonal deal festivals.
Wima's Ghana version
Wima's pain stories write themselves — the relative who "managed" the money, the quote that left out 30% of the cost. Dramatise it, then be the fix. Run WhatsApp catalogs and Detty-December deal drops.
ZillowUSA · 50%+ of portal traffic
What they did
Made browsing entertainment — "Zillow surfing" became a pastime and fan-made Zillow Gone Wild a TV show. Gave away a free data tool (Zestimate) as the hook, and owned the data.
Wima's Ghana version
Post dream builds and "you won't believe this Ghanaian home" content for the window-shoppers — they share and become the audience. Make a free build-cost/BOQ tool Wima's Zestimate, and own the data every use generates.
Duolingo16M+ TikTok followers
What they did
Took until their 39th video to hit — and broke through only when they accepted "people want to be entertained, not sold to." Gave a personality (Duo) a human face, and married paid + organic.
Wima's Ghana version
Entertain first, sell second. Make Jamal and Gershon the human faces (Wima's Duo). Iterate relentlessly — don't quit at post 38. Boost the organic winners with paid; never pay to push a flop.
WhatsApp Commerce LeadersTata CLiQ · Indian fashion · Unilever
What they did
An Indian fashion brand went WhatsApp-first — catalog 24h before the website — for ~45% higher launch-day revenue. Tata CLiQ was useful first (order updates), then sold, for ~10x ROI. WhatsApp sees ~98% open rates.
Wima's Ghana version
Drop every Deal Hub deal to the WhatsApp community first — subscribers feel like insiders. Lead with utility (price updates, tips), then the offer. Ghana is the ideal market for this — 91.8% WhatsApp penetration.
Infra.MarketIndia · ~$2B on aggregation
What they did
Built a $2B construction-materials business by pooling small contractors' orders to save buyers 5–7% — proving demand aggregation in a fragmented market like Ghana's.
Wima's Ghana version
Make Deal Hub's social message concrete: "Buy together, save 5–10%." Post the running group-buy count; the bigger the pool, the better the price — social content that literally sells the aggregation.
Section 03 Platform Playbooks

Five Platforms,
Five Different Jobs.

Each platform does one job in the funnel. Post native to each — never copy-paste the same clip everywhere. Percentages are Ghana-specific reach; the job is what that platform is for in Wima's system.

WhatsApp91.8% of GH internet users · 98% open rateJob: CONVERT
The closing rail — where every other platform sends people. Status (free, reaches all saved contacts): before/after, weekly price update, social proof. Broadcast lists (opt-in only): deal drops, BOQ turnarounds. Community groups: builders' tips + Q&A. Launch every Deal Hub deal here first. Reply within minutes — speed is a competitive advantage. Consent is non-negotiable.
Facebook~66% of GH social share · groups are goldJob: COMMUNITY + REACH
Where Ghana's building conversations happen. Post as Gershon personally in groups, not as a faceless page — community members respond to people. Lead with questions (3–5× the comments of statements) and educational guides. Target diaspora groups ("Ghanaians in UK Building in Ghana") separately. This is the organic engine that warms the audience before any ad spend.
TikTok~70% of GH internet usersJob: DISCOVERY
The Duolingo lesson lives here: entertain, don't sell. Fast before/afters, "which ceiling?" explainers, satisfying install clips, trend-jacking within 48 hours. Hook in 3 seconds, no logo up front. This is where strangers who've never heard of Wima discover it. Iterate relentlessly — most won't hit; keep going.
InstagramReels + carousels · aspirationalJob: INSPIRE + PROOF
The visual portfolio. Before/after Reels (post 6–9pm Ghana time for 34% more reach), carousels ("5 signs your building needs insulation"), finished-project galleries. Keyword-rich captions + hashtags so posts stay searchable. This is Wima's Houzz-style save-worthy library.
LinkedInUntapped in GH construction · B2BJob: SUPPLIERS + BANKS
The rail almost no Ghana builder uses — which is the opportunity. Founder-led posts from Gershon (turn the strategy docs into insight posts), data & market posts (PBCI, cost trends), and warming bank partners, suppliers, and professionals for weeks before any pitch. Slow, compounding, high-value.
