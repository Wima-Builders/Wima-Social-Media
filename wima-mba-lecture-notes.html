The Content System · Part Of The MBA

Six content pillars — each one answers a pain

This is where content becomes part of the MBA. Every post Wima publishes belongs to one of six pillars, held at a 3:1 value-to-offer ratio — and each pillar exists to resolve a documented client fear. Content is not decoration; it's the growth engine.

Pillar 01 · Value

Educate

Cost breakdowns, the build order, "mistakes that cost twice," how to read a BOQ.

→ Answers: hidden costs, bad estimates, misinformation
Pillar 02 · Value

Inspire

Before/after transformations, finished reveals — the most-shared home format.

→ Answers: "will it even look good / be worth it?"
Pillar 03 · Value

People

Jamal, master craftsmen, featured consultants. A human face on the brand.

→ Answers: "who's actually coming to my site?"
Pillar 04 · Value

Entertain

Dream builds, "you won't believe this Ghana home" — content browsers share.

→ Answers: top-of-funnel reach; builds the audience
Pillar 05 · Value

Proof

Real clients, real voice notes, milestone photos, case studies with numbers.

→ Answers: fear of betrayal, being cheated, stalled builds
Pillar 06 · Offer

Offer

Free-BOQ CTA, Deal Hub drops — WhatsApp-first, sparingly, to a warm audience.

→ Answers: cost fear, material price volatility

Implementation · Tie It Together

The scaling sequence for Wima + Deal Hub

The order that compounds

1 · Platform + Content (Lectures 1–2) build the audience. 2 · Organic + Community (Lectures 3, 6) turn the audience into a trusting community on WhatsApp. 3 · Paid + LinkedIn (Lectures 4–5) pour fuel on what works and reach suppliers and banks. 4 · AI + Analytics (Lectures 7–8) let one team output like ten and keep only what pays. 5 · Capstone (Lecture 9) launches Deal Hub into the community you built. Community first; Deal Hub second.

Where marketing lifts it further

The Marketing track wraps the social engine in the moves that raise Wima's whole standing: a sharp position (the honest builder), outcome-and-group pricing (Cool-Ceiling + Deal Hub), a free-BOQ funnel, bank/supplier/professional partnerships, earned-media authority (B&FT, MyJoyOnline), engineered referral, and real-world growth activities — build clinics, a showroom, diaspora webinars, fairs, a tendering desk — expanding Accra → Tema → Kumasi. Social wins the audience; marketing turns it into a market position.

The one line to hold

Duolingo took 39 videos to land a hit. The lesson isn't the 39th video — it's that they didn't stop at the 38th. Learn it Monday, ship it Friday, measure it Sunday, and don't quit before it compounds.
