Module 00

How social really works

Before any tactic: understand what you're actually playing. Every platform is an attention auction. The algorithm has one job — keep people on the app — so it rewards whatever holds attention. Master that and everything else follows.

The algorithm's real job

It doesn't care about you, your brand, or your product. It measures one thing: does your content keep people watching, and do they react? Watch-time, completion, shares, saves, comments — these tell it "show this to more people." Reach is earned by holding attention, never demanded.

Organic vs paid — the relationship

Organic proves what people want; paid amplifies what already works. Never pay to push content that failed organically — you'll just buy a bigger failure. The workflow is always: post free → find the winners → put money behind the winners.

The creator mindset

People don't open TikTok to see adverts. They come to be entertained, taught, or moved. Wima wins by being a useful media brand that happens to sell, not a seller that happens to post. Value first, always — the selling is earned.

The compounding truth

Social rewards consistency more than brilliance. One viral post fades; a reliable cadence trains both the algorithm and the audience to expect you. Showing up 4× a week for a year beats one perfect post.

Module 01

The Wima channel map — which platform does which job

Don't post the same thing everywhere. Each platform has a distinct role in Ghana's mix. Match the message to the platform's job.

TikTok & Reels
Discovery engine · reaches ~70% of users

Where strangers find you. Short build-education video, hook-first. This is your top-of-funnel reach machine — and the one competitors are weakest on. Post most here.

WhatsApp
Community & conversion rail · 91.8% of internet users

Not a megaphone — a relationship and closing tool. Broadcast lists, community groups, Status, and click-to-chat. This is where trust deepens and deals close. The most important rail you own.

Facebook
Broad reach + groups · 77.4% · older & diaspora

Wide reach, strong for the 35+ and diaspora audiences, and Groups build community. Great for longer posts, launches, and event promotion.

Instagram
Design & aspiration · Wima Signature

The visual showcase: before/after, finishes, Signature interiors. Reels here double as TikTok reach.

YouTube
Long-form authority · evergreen search

Deep build guides and site walkthroughs that rank in search for years — the video twin of the blog. Slower, but compounds.

The one funnel to hold in your head

TikTok / Reels / Facebook (a stranger discovers you) → follow & engage (they trust you) → WhatsApp (you build the relationship) → Deal Hub / tender / finance (they buy). Every post should move someone one step along this path. If a post does nothing for the funnel, it's decoration.

Module 02

The content engine — free posting that spreads

Consistent, useful content is the entire organic game. Here's the system that produces it without burning out — and the formats proven to work for building content.

The three jobs every post does one of

Reach content

Broad, hooky, shareable. Designed to travel to strangers. "The mistake that cost this builder GHS 40,000."

Nurture content

Teaches, builds trust, shows expertise. "How to verify a land title, step by step."

Convert content

Deals, launches, clear CTAs. Used sparingly. "This week only: drywall ceiling kit at pool price."

The ratio: roughly 3 value posts (reach + nurture) for every 1 sell post. Earn attention far more than you spend it, or the algorithm and the audience both tune you out.

Formats proven for build content

Rotate these so you never run out of ideas:

The costly mistake / warning"How much does X cost" breakdownBefore & after revealMyth-bustStep-by-step processSite walk-throughDiaspora scam storyMaterial comparisonAsk-the-expert Q&A"Don't build until you watch this"
The hook — your single most important skill

The first 3 seconds decide everything. If the opening line doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters — the best content in the world dies behind a weak hook. Lead with the hook, not the logo. Movement, curiosity, or a bold claim beats branding every time.

Hook patterns (fill in the blank)

  • "The #1 mistake Ghanaians make when building a ___ "
  • "Never pay a mason before you ___ "
  • "Building from abroad? Watch this before you send money."
  • "This costs GHS ___ . Here's why most people pay double."
  • "Stop using ___ for your ceiling. Do this instead."
Practice drillTake one Deal Hub product and write 10 different hooks for it in 15 minutes. Ten. The skill is fluency — the more hooks you can generate, the more winners you'll find.

The production system — batch, don't scramble

  • Batch by theme. Film 6–8 videos in one session, not one a day. Consistency comes from a stock, not daily inspiration.
  • Repurpose one idea 4 ways. A single "cost to roof a house" idea becomes a TikTok, a Reel, a Facebook post, a WhatsApp Status, and a blog section. One insight, five placements.
  • Harvest the business. Every Deal Hub quote, every Jamal install, every diaspora client's question is content. You're not inventing — you're documenting real work.
  • Cadence: aim for 3–5 posts per week on TikTok minimum; that feeds the algorithm without burning the team.
Module 03

Writing for social — hooks, captions, CTAs

Words are how attention becomes action. Same craft as the hook, extended to the whole post.

The caption formula

Hook line (stop the scroll) → value (the useful bit) → one clear CTA (comment, save, or "message us on WhatsApp"). One caption, one job. Never bury the point.

Write like you talk

Plain, specific, Ghanaian. "This will crack your wall in one rainy season" beats "suboptimal materials may compromise structural integrity." Specificity is trust.

The CTA discipline

Tell people exactly what to do next, and only one thing. "Comment BUILD and we'll send the cost guide." A post with no CTA is a dead end for the funnel.

Curiosity & stakes

Open loops ("most people get step 3 completely wrong") and name the stakes (money lost, time wasted, dreams stalled). People act to avoid loss more than to chase gain.

Module 04

Organic growth mechanics — 0 to 100k

Growth isn't luck. It's engineering the signals the algorithm rewards, then compounding them.

The signals that grow you (in order)

Shares (the strongest — content people send to friends), then saves, completion rate, comments, then likes (weakest). Design content to be shared and saved — "send this to someone building a house."

The completion trick

Shorter videos that get fully watched beat longer ones that get abandoned. If your completion rate is below ~40%, tighten the hook and cut the fat before anything else.

Trends & sounds

Using a trending sound or format gives the algorithm a reason to push you. Adapt build content onto trends — that's the bridge to Module 05.

Engage back, fast

Reply to every comment in the first hour — it signals a lively post and pulls reach. Your comment section is content too; the best reply can outperform the post.

The follower ladder — how each stage is actually won

0 → 1K
Be genuinely useful. One "mistakes" video that gets shared. Post daily-ish, reply to everyone. No spend needed.
1K → 10K
Consistency + video. Relentless cadence, trend-jacking, the diaspora group as referral engine.
10K → 50K
Become the reference. The price-tracker data asset earns shares & press; collaborations widen reach.
50K → 100K
Ubiquity. You're the name people tag under "how do I build?" Search, community & video reinforce each other.

WhatsApp growth (the conversion rail)

  • Broadcast lists & community groups — regional builder groups + a diaspora build-support group. Every video ends with "join the builders' group."
  • Status — daily deal drops and tips to warm contacts.
  • The rules: only message people who opted in; never spam; lead with value. WhatsApp trust is fragile and irreplaceable.
Module 05

Ethical ambush marketing — borrow attention, earn trust honestly

Ambush marketing means showing up where attention already is, instead of paying to build it from scratch. Done ethically, it's one of the highest-leverage skills in social — clever, not sleazy. The line is simple: borrow the moment honestly; never deceive.

The tactics

Trend-jacking & sound-jacking

Attach build content to a trending sound, format, or challenge on TikTok. The trend carries your reach; your angle carries your message. Fast is everything — trends have a 48-hour window.

News-jacking

When cedi news, a cement-price change, or a housing-policy story breaks, publish the useful builder's take that day. You ride the search and conversation spike with genuine value.

Moment & season marketing

Rainy season → roofing & waterproofing. Harmattan → wall cracks. Festive season → "finish your build before Christmas." Year of Return → diaspora building. Ghana's calendar is a content calendar.

Comment-section presence

Leave the single most useful comment on big news pages, influencers', and yes, competitors' build posts — where your audience already gathers. Add real value; people click the helpful stranger's profile.

Competitor-adjacent (the honest way)

Don't attack — out-serve. Answer the exact questions competitors skip, and target their interest-audiences with better content. Be the more useful voice in the same conversation.

Event & expo riding

Around property fairs and building expos, publish relevant content and be present in the hashtag — without falsely implying you're an official sponsor. Ride the attention, don't fake the affiliation.

✓ Ethical — always
  • Add genuine value everywhere you show up
  • Be transparent about who you are
  • Move fast on real moments and trends
  • Use public conversations and open hashtags
  • Out-teach competitors; win on usefulness
  • Credit sounds/creators you build on
✕ Never — this is the sleazy line
  • No fake accounts or astroturfed reviews/comments
  • No impersonating a competitor, person, or event
  • No falsely claiming sponsorship or affiliation
  • No hijacking a trademark/hashtag to mislead
  • No comment spam or copy-paste dumping
  • No buying followers; no misleading claims
Why the ethics aren't optional for Wima

Wima's entire wedge is trust. A single deceptive stunt that gets exposed undoes years of it. Ethical ambush works precisely because it pairs borrowed attention with real value — that's what converts a stranger's glance into a follower. Cross the line and you win a moment but lose the brand.

Module 06

Paid ads — the machine

Paid is a multiplier, not a magic wand. It scales content that already works and puts you in front of exact audiences — including the diaspora — on demand. Here's the whole system.

The structure (identical on Meta & TikTok)

Campaign
The objective. What you want — reach, video views, traffic, leads, or messages. Pick one goal per campaign.
Ad set
The audience, budget & placement. Who sees it, how much you spend, and where it shows.
Ad
The creative. The actual video/image + caption. Test several per ad set.

Objectives mapped to the funnel

  • Top (cold): Reach / Video Views — cheap attention, build a retargeting pool.
  • Mid (warm): Traffic / Engagement — to the blog or profile.
  • Bottom (hot): Leads / Conversions / Messages (click-to-WhatsApp) — drive real action.
Wima's killer ad format

Click-to-WhatsApp (Messages) ads. They drop a prospect straight into your conversion rail — cheap, native to how Ghanaians already transact, and they open a real conversation instead of a cold form. For a WhatsApp-first business, this is the single highest-value paid format. Make it your default bottom-funnel campaign.

Targeting — where paid earns its keep

Cold audiences

Interests (real estate, home improvement, DIY), behaviours, and geography — Greater Accra, Tema, Kumasi. Let the algorithm find buyers with a broad, well-fed audience.

The diaspora goldmine

Target Ghanaians in the UK, US, Canada by location + interest. High-value, USD-earning, under-served — and your Procurement Desk is the offer. Almost nobody targets diaspora builders specifically.

Custom audiences (retargeting)

People who watched your videos, visited the blog, or messaged you. The highest-ROI spend there is — warm people convert far cheaper than cold.

Lookalikes

Once you have buyers or engagers, tell the platform "find more people like these." Compounds as your data grows.

Budgeting — start small, scale winners

Monthly budget = target results × target cost per result + 20–30% testing buffer
  • Start with small daily tests (a few dollars per ad set). Platform minimums are low — roughly $50/campaign and $20/ad-set on TikTok.
  • Don't scale until you have a winner. Find the ad that converts cheaply, then increase budget on that one gradually (raising too fast resets the algorithm's learning).
  • Shift testing into cheaper months; costs rise 15–40% in Q4 (festive season).

The Ghana reality

Those are US/global figures. Ghana and emerging African markets run well below them — often a fraction of US CPMs — because the auction is less crowded. So treat the table as guardrails, not targets: run a small test, read your own CPM/CPC/cost-per-message, and let real numbers set your budget. Also note reported cost is often 20–40% higher than true cost due to tracking gaps — don't overreact to the dashboard.

Creative wins over spend

A native-feeling, hook-first video costs far less to run than a polished advert, because the platform rewards ads people actually watch with cheaper delivery. Boost your organic winners (Spark Ads on TikTok / boosting a proven post on Meta) rather than making "ad-looking" ads from scratch.

Module 07

Measurement & iteration

What you don't measure, you can't improve — and what you measure wrong, you'll optimise into the ground. Track the signals that map to money.

Metrics that matter

Organic: completion rate, shares, saves, follower growth, WhatsApp joins. Paid: cost per result (per message, per lead), ROAS, CTR. Everything else is diagnostics.

Vanity vs value

Likes and views feel good but don't pay. A video with 5,000 views and 40 WhatsApp messages beats one with 500,000 views and none. Judge by funnel movement, not applause.

The weekly review

Every week: what were the top 3 posts by completion & shares? Rebuild next week around what worked. Kill what didn't. This loop is the whole job.

Test one thing at a time

Change the hook OR the audience OR the creative — never all three — or you won't know what moved the number. Disciplined testing is how amateurs become experts.

Module 08

The Wima playbook — applied to the Dry Wall launch

Everything above, put to work on the first product launch (Dry Wall with Jamal), so you see how the pieces combine.

Weeks before — organic warm-up

Publish the drywall education content (aligned to the SEO map): "POP vs drywall," install demos, "the ceiling mistake that costs you twice." Build the audience the launch will convert. Seed the WhatsApp builders' group.

Ethical ambush layer

Ride any ceiling/finishing trend or sound; news-jack any cement/material price story; leave the best comment on home-renovation posts. Post the drywall demo on a trending format.

Launch — convert content + paid

Announce the deal. Run click-to-WhatsApp ads to Accra/Tema/Kumasi + diaspora, boosting the best-performing organic demo video. Retarget everyone who watched the education content.

Measure & feed forward

Track cost-per-message and orders, not views. The launch throws off real stories and numbers — which become the next round of education content. The loop turns.

Then repeat, in sequence

Same playbook for Kabinart (kitchens), then the Aquarium division — in that locked order. Never conflate launches; each gets its own warm-up, ambush layer, and paid push.

Module 09

The path to expert — a 90-day regimen

You don't become expert by reading — you become expert by shipping, measuring, and adjusting. Here's the practice loop that gets you there in one quarter.

Weeks 1–2
Foundations & swipe file

Work Modules 0–4. Start a swipe file: save 30 build/finance videos that stopped your scroll and note why. Post your first 6 videos — badly is fine. Shipping beats perfect.

Weeks 3–4
Hooks & cadence

10 hooks a day, every day. Lock a 4×/week posting cadence. Reply to every comment. Watch your completion rates and rebuild around your top 3 posts.

Weeks 5–8
Ambush & community

Add Module 5: ride 2 trends and 1 news moment a week; leave 5 value-comments a day. Grow the WhatsApp group. Aim for your first shareable "mistakes" hit.

Weeks 9–12
Paid & proof

Run your first small click-to-WhatsApp campaign on a proven organic video. Test one audience vs one diaspora audience. Read your real numbers. Scale the winner. You're now operating, not learning.

Free resources to go deeper

Meta Blueprint (free official ads courses) and TikTok Creative Center / Academy (trending sounds, formats, ad training) are the two best free sources. But treat them as reference — reps on real Wima content teach you more than any course.
